I think New Zealand will approach the tournament with a disciplined, defensive mindset. Their strength lies in their ability to stay compact and frustrate opponents, leveraging their defensive solidity and physical presence. I expect them to be well-organised, making it difficult for teams to break them down through the middle. Counter-attacks, often initiated by Joe Bell's distribution or quick transitions, will be crucial. The role of Chris Wood as a target man, not just for goals but also holding up play, is indispensable. Set pieces, both offensive and defensive, are another area where I believe they can excel due to their height and commitment. Their attacking impetus, however, could be limited, often relying on moments of individual brilliance from Singh or direct play to Wood. I foresee them being a tough team to beat, but also one that might struggle to consistently create clear-cut chances.
