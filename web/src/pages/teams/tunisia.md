I think Tunisia will approach the 2026 World Cup with their characteristic defensive solidity as the foundation. I expect them to be incredibly difficult to break down, organized, and disciplined, especially through the spine of the team with Skhiri shielding the defense. Their strong defensive unit and midfield engine room, marshaled by players like Laïdouni, will be key to frustrating opponents and absorbing pressure effectively. However, I anticipate their primary challenge will be converting defensive resilience into consistent offensive threat. While players like Achouri and the emerging Hannibal Mejbri offer flashes of brilliance, I think the team often struggles with sustained creative output in the final third and a prolific goalscorer. I expect them to rely heavily on quick transitions and set-pieces to grab crucial goals, aiming to keep matches tight and snatch results. It will be a battle of attrition, leaning on their strong defensive identity.
