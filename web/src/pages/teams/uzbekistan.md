I think Uzbekistan under Srečko Katanec has developed into a formidable outfit, blending tactical discipline with individual flair. I expect their 4-3-3 formation to provide a solid midfield base for quick transitions. The pace of Fayzullaev and Urunov on the wings, combined with Shomurodov's target man presence, creates a potent attacking trident. Their ability to switch quickly from defense to attack will be key. However, I believe they might struggle with defensive solidity against truly elite World Cup sides, particularly when facing sustained pressure. They need to ensure their midfield can shield the backline effectively to avoid being overrun. Set-pieces could also be an area to exploit, both offensively and defensively. Overall, I expect them to be a spirited dark horse, capable of surprising opponents on their day.
