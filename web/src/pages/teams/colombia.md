I think Colombia comes into the 2026 World Cup as an intriguing dark horse, capable of surprising many. I expect to see them line up in a dynamic 4-3-3 formation, explicitly designed to maximize their incredible pace and flair on the flanks. The likes of Luis Díaz and Luis Sinisterra will be absolutely pivotal, constantly stretching defenses and providing direct attacking outlets. In midfield, I believe Jefferson Lerma's defensive acumen will provide a crucial shield, allowing Jhon Arias and Matheus Uribe to drive forward and link play effectively. However, I also perceive some potential vulnerabilities. While offensively potent, I think their defensive unit, particularly with overlapping full-backs, could be exposed on quick counter-attacks. The delicate balance between their aggressive attacking play and maintaining consistent defensive solidity will be absolutely key to their progression. I expect their ability to transition rapidly from defense to attack to be a major weapon, but against truly elite opposition, their defensive organization might be severely tested. Colombia thrives on passion and individual brilliance, and I think that will define their identity on the world stage.
