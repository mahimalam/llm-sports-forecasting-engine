I think Brazil enters the 2026 World Cup as a formidable force, especially in attack. Their 4-3-3 formation allows for incredible fluidity, with Vinicius Jr. and Raphinha providing devastating width and direct goal threat, while Rodrygo masterfully links midfield to attack with his intelligent movement. I expect Bruno Guimarães to anchor the midfield, dictating tempo and providing crucial defensive cover, allowing Lucas Paquetá to surge forward and influence play in the final third. However, I believe their defensive solidity, particularly when the full-backs commit high, could be tested by disciplined counter-attacking teams. While Marquinhos and Militão are top-tier, the team's overall balance will be paramount. Their ability to transition from defense to breathtaking attack is undeniable, but I think they'll need to develop more consistent scoring patterns beyond individual brilliance to truly dominate the toughest matches.
