I think Mexico for the 2026 World Cup will be a fascinating team, especially as co-hosts. I expect their tactical setup to be built around a solid midfield base, anchored by Edson Álvarez, providing a platform for their dynamic wingers to excel. The transition play, with players like Hirving Lozano and Julián Quiñones, will be a significant weapon, allowing them to exploit space quickly. I see Santiago Giménez becoming a pivotal figure upfront, providing the clinical edge they've sometimes lacked. However, I believe defensive consistency remains a challenge. While individually talented, the backline can be vulnerable against elite attacking units. I expect to see moments of brilliant link-up play and passionate displays, but the key will be maintaining focus and tactical discipline throughout 90 minutes. Converting dominance into goals, particularly against compact defenses, will also be crucial for their progress in the tournament.
