I think Senegal approaches World Cup 2026 with a solid foundation, blending experienced leaders with exciting young talent. Their strength lies in dynamic wide players, particularly Sadio Mané and Ismaila Sarr, who can unlock defenses with pace and skill. The defensive spine, featuring Koulibaly, offers formidable protection, while Pape Matar Sarr and Lamine Camara in midfield provide energetic link-up play. I believe their tactical approach will heavily leverage rapid transitions and strong pressing to exploit opponents. However, I think questions remain about their creative depth if faced with a resilient, low block, and the endurance of some key older players across a demanding tournament schedule will be crucial.
