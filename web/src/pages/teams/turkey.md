I think Turkey heads into World Cup 2026 as an intriguing dark horse, built around a technically gifted midfield. Hakan Çalhanoğlu, Orkun Kökçü, and the burgeoning talent of Arda Güler and Kenan Yıldız offer immense creativity and control in the center of the park. Their ability to dictate tempo and unlock defenses, especially through set-pieces, will be crucial. I expect Ferdi Kadıoğlu to be a key outlet on the left, providing dynamism and defensive cover. The main challenge, I believe, will be maintaining defensive solidity against top-tier attacks and finding consistent goals from a central striker. If they can tighten up at the back and Enes Ünal finds his clinical edge, this team has the potential to surprise many and advance deep into the tournament.
