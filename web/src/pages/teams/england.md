I think England arrive at the 2026 World Cup with arguably their strongest attacking lineup in decades. The blend of Harry Kane's prolific finishing, complemented by the explosive wide play of Bukayo Saka and Marcus Rashford, gives them a serious threat. I expect the midfield trio of Declan Rice, Jude Bellingham, and Phil Foden to be particularly dominant; Rice anchoring, Bellingham driving forward with immense power, and Foden providing that creative spark. However, I believe there are still questions defensively, particularly in managing transitions when the full-backs are high up the pitch. While John Stones is excellent, the partnership alongside him will be crucial. I think their ability to maintain defensive solidity against top-tier attacks, especially in knockout stages, will ultimately determine how far they progress.
