I think Scotland under Steve Clarke has forged a truly formidable identity, built fundamentally on defensive resilience and an incredible team spirit. They're a side that makes themselves incredibly difficult to beat, and I expect that foundation to be critical in 2026. The midfield, led by McTominay and McGinn, offers an immense work rate and a surprising threat breaking forward. However, I believe their main challenge will remain converting chances. I don't see a prolific goalscorer emerging just yet, which places significant pressure on midfielders and wide players to contribute. I expect to see them frustrate bigger teams, leveraging their set-piece prowess. They'll need their key players, especially Robertson, to deliver consistently to navigate a challenging group stage. I think their defensive strength gives them a fighting chance against anyone.
