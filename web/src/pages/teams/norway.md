I think Norway will enter World Cup 2026 with cautious optimism, largely banking on their stellar attacking duo. The 4-3-3 formation allows Martin Ødegaard to dictate play from midfield, feeding Erling Haaland who I expect to be clinical as ever. Their strength lies in swift transitions and direct attacks, capitalizing on Haaland's pace and power. However, I anticipate challenges in deeper defensive phases against elite teams, particularly if their midfield isn't perfectly disciplined. Squad depth could also be a concern if injuries strike key players. Ultimately, Norway's success will hinge on individual brilliance complemented by a cohesive defensive effort, which I believe is still a work in progress.
