I think Bosnia-Herzegovina will approach the World Cup with a pragmatic mindset, focusing on defensive solidity as their foundation. I expect them to be well-organized, absorbing pressure and looking to capitalize on set-pieces or quick transitions. The backline, marshaled by Ahmedhodžić, looks robust, and the midfield trio possesses the technical quality to retain possession and dictate tempo in phases. However, I believe their main challenge will be consistently converting chances. While Demirović offers a physical presence, I'm not convinced there's enough cutting edge across the front three to break down stubborn defenses regularly. They'll need their full-backs to provide significant attacking impetus. Ultimately, I anticipate a disciplined team that will be tough to beat, but perhaps lacking the offensive firepower to truly threaten the tournament's stronger sides, making group stage progression a significant achievement.
