I think Czechia will arrive at the World Cup as a well-drilled unit, focusing heavily on their defensive solidity. I expect their backline, marshaled by robust centre-backs and the tireless Tomáš Souček in midfield, to be extremely difficult to break down. They'll aim to frustrate opponents, absorbing pressure and then launching attacks, often leveraging their significant aerial threat from set pieces and crosses. However, I feel their attacking play in open spaces might lack consistent incisiveness. While Patrik Schick is a proven finisher, I anticipate they might struggle to create a high volume of clear-cut chances from intricate build-up play. Their success will likely hinge on winning the midfield battle through sheer work rate and capitalizing on specific moments, rather than dominating possession or overwhelming teams with fluid attacking movements.
