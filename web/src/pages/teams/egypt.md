I think Egypt will approach the World Cup with a pragmatic yet dangerous style. Defensively, I expect them to be well-organized and difficult to break down, leveraging their strong central defenders and disciplined midfield. Their primary attacking threat, of course, revolves around Mohamed Salah, who I anticipate will be given a free role to exploit spaces and punish defenses on the break. The transition play will be crucial, as I believe they will look to absorb pressure and then release their quick wide players and target man. While they possess individual quality in attack, I think their challenge will be consistently creating chances against top-tier opposition when possession is demanded of them. The midfield will need to be resilient, shielding the defense and facilitating quick turnovers to unleash their star forward.
