I think Qatar will approach the 2026 World Cup with a familiar, disciplined structure, prioritizing defensive solidity. Their 4-3-3 formation allows for quick transitions, especially utilizing the pace and skill of Akram Afif on the left flank. I expect them to sit deep against stronger opponents, absorbing pressure and looking to exploit any spaces on the counter. The midfield trio, anchored by a deep-lying playmaker, will be crucial in disrupting opposition play and initiating attacks, connecting defense to a mobile front three. While they possess good team chemistry and an experienced core, I anticipate challenges in consistently breaking down well-organized defenses. Their success will likely hinge on individual moments of brilliance from their key attackers and their ability to maintain tactical discipline throughout the full ninety minutes against top-tier opposition.
