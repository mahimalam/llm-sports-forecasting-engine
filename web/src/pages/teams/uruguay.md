Uruguay in 2026, I believe, will be a formidable unit under Marcelo Bielsa's continued influence. I expect them to play with intense pressing and rapid transitions, leveraging the incredible athleticism of players like Valverde and Núñez. Their midfield, with Ugarte providing steel and Bentancur offering technical ability, looks incredibly well-balanced. Defensively, Araújo and Giménez form a robust partnership, but I think the full-backs might be asked to contribute heavily in attack, occasionally leaving them exposed. Uruguay's primary threat will come from their blistering pace on the counter, where I anticipate Núñez will be devastating. They might, however, struggle against teams that can maintain possession and break down a low block with intricate passing, occasionally lacking that final creative touch themselves.
