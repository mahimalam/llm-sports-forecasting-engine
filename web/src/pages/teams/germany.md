I think Germany under Nagelsmann will present a formidable challenge at the 2026 World Cup. The attacking midfield trio of Florian Wirtz and Jamal Musiala, combined with dynamic wide forwards like Leroy Sané and Kai Havertz, offers immense creativity and unpredictability. I expect their fluid movement, intelligent interchangeability, and quick transitions to be a nightmare for opposition defenses, especially with Kimmich pushing forward from right-back. Defensively, Antonio Rüdiger provides crucial leadership and aggression, anchoring the backline. However, I believe they might occasionally struggle with true defensive stability when facing rapid, direct counter-attacks, particularly if their fullbacks are caught high up the pitch. The reliance on Niclas Füllkrug for consistent goalscoring is also something I’ll be watching closely; converting chances will be paramount. Overall, I anticipate a highly entertaining and offensively potent side that could go deep in the tournament.
